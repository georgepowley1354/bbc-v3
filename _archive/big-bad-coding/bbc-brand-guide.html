Middle B Color — Reasoning

#3d4a5c is not a neutral gray. It sits at hue 215° — the exact same chromatic axis as the electric blue C (#0066FF).

Desaturated to 20% saturation and darkened to 30% luminance, it reads as "structural dark" at a distance, but reveals blue undertones up close — signaling tech craft and trust.

A flat gray (#555, #444) would sever the chromatic thread between white and blue. #3d4a5c earns its position as the connective tissue of the BBC mark.

03 — Logo Usage Rules

Do

✓

Overlap is intentional. B2 (slate) sits 15–20% behind B1 and C. Never separate the letters fully.

✓

Use on #080808 dark backgrounds as primary. The mark is designed for dark-first.

✓

On light backgrounds, swap B1 (white) to near-black (#0d0d0d). Keep B2 and C unchanged.

✓

Maintain clear space equal to the cap height of the letter "C" on all sides of the mark.

✓

Minimum size: 20px cap height for digital, 12mm for print.

Do Not

✗

Do not recolor the letters. B1=white/dark, B2=#3d4a5c, C=#0066FF — fixed. No gradients on letters.

✗

Do not remove the overlap. Spacing the letters apart destroys the connective tension of the mark.

✗

Do not change the stacking order. B2 must always be the deepest layer.

✗

Do not use on mid-tone backgrounds (#555–#999) — neither the white nor the dark version has sufficient contrast.

✗

Do not stretch or condense the letters independently. Scale uniformly only.

06 — Competitive Positioning

How BBC visually differs from the 2026 dev tool branding standard:

Vercel

Triangle mark. Monochrome black/white. Ultra-minimal. Platform-agnostic.

Linear

Abstract orbital mark. Purple gradient. Sleek, spatial. Product-first.

Resend

Letterform R. Single color. Clean, functional. Tool-focused.

Clerk

Soft gradient sphere. Friendly rounded. Security-meets-accessibility.

Big Bad Coding

3-letter overlap mark. Three-color system. Bold, condensed, confident. Human agency behind the code.

Where others lean on abstraction or pure geometry, BBC uses letterform identity — the brand IS the initials, stacked and in tension. The overlap communicates collaboration and craft without a single metaphor. It stands apart precisely because it doesn't chase minimalism.
