# Brief Framework

## Discovery Question Set

Use these in the discovery call. Not every question for every project — read the room and prioritize based on what you don't know yet.

### Business Context
- What does your business do, and who is your primary customer?
- What's working right now in how you attract and convert clients?
- What's broken or frustrating about your current web presence?
- Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?

### Project Scope
- What do you want someone to be able to do on your new site?
- Are there pages or sections you know you need?
- Do you have existing content (copy, photos, video) or do we need to create it?
- Are there sites you've seen that you liked? Why?

### Technical
- Do you have a domain? Is it registered somewhere?
- Do you have hosting? Or is that something we're setting up?
- Do you need a contact form? A booking system? A payment flow?
- Do you have a CMS or do you want to manage content yourself after launch?

### Timeline & Budget
- Do you have a hard launch date, or is this flexible?
- What's your budget range for this project?
- Are there any other vendors we'll need to coordinate with (photographer, copywriter, etc.)?

```markdown
- Pillars: [S1? S2? S3? S4?]
- Estimated tier: [Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3]
- Timeline: [hard date or flexible]
- Budget signal: [range mentioned or inferred]

[Domain, hosting, integrations needed]

## Content Status
[Do they have copy? Photos? Anything we need to create?]

## Red Flags
[Anything that signals scope creep risk or unclear expectations]

## Next Step
[What we agreed to do next]
```

## SOW Structure

A BBC scope of work has 6 sections:

1. **Project Overview** — One paragraph: who the client is, what we're building, why
2. **Deliverables** — Explicit list, numbered. No vague items. ("4 pages: homepage, about, services, contact")
3. **What's Not Included** — Explicitly exclude things the client might assume are in scope
4. **Timeline** — Key milestones with dates. Discovery, design review, build complete, launch
5. **Investment** — Total cost, payment schedule, deposit requirement
6. **Terms** — Revision rounds (number), approval process, change order policy, kill fee

---

## Proposal Review Checklist

Before sending any proposal, verify:

- [ ] Opens with the client's problem, not BBC's credentials
- [ ] No agency jargon or filler phrases
- [ ] Deliverables are specific and enumerable
- [ ] Timeline has concrete dates
- [ ] Investment section is clear — no hidden costs
- [ ] "What's not included" is present
- [ ] Voice matches `voice-and-tone.md` rules
- [ ] Short sentences, active voice throughout
- [ ] Proposal is under 2 pages for Tier 1, under 3 for Tier 2+
