# Service Pillars

BBC's work organizes around 6 pillars. Most projects span 2-3 pillars.

---

## S1 — Brand & Identity

**What it is:** Logo, color system, typography, brand guidelines. The visual foundation everything else is built on.

**When it applies:** New businesses, businesses rebranding, businesses whose current look no longer fits what they do.

**What it includes:**
- Logo design (primary + variations)
- Color palette (primary, secondary, accent, neutral)
- Typography selection (heading + body + optional display)
- Brand guidelines document (how to use the above)

**Example projects:** doberts-canvas (artist brand identity), kris-mid-city (restaurant rebrand)

**What it is NOT:** A full marketing strategy. Brand & identity is visual system, not messaging framework.

---

## S2 — Web Design

**What it is:** UX/UI design for web — wireframes, layouts, responsive design, visual design in Figma before a line of code is written.

**When it applies:** Any project where the client wants to see and approve the design before the build starts. Recommended for all new site builds.

**What it includes:**
- Wireframes (low-fidelity layout structure)
- Full visual design in Figma (desktop + mobile)
- Design review and iteration
- Handoff-ready design specs

**Example projects:** whatsshakinbacon (landing page + menu), remodelnow (services pages)

**What it is NOT:** A substitute for a brand identity — if the client doesn't have logos and colors locked, S1 comes first.

---

## S3 — Web Development

**What it is:** Custom code. We build in Astro or Next.js depending on the project. Static-first, component-based, fast.

**When it applies:** Any project that needs a real site built — not a template editor, not Wix, not a drag-and-drop builder.

**What it includes:**
- Custom component architecture
- Responsive implementation
- CMS integration (Sanity, Contentful, or simple markdown depending on scope)
- Performance optimization (Core Web Vitals)
- Deploy to Netlify or Cloudflare Pages

**Example projects:** bbc-v3 (agency site), kris-mid-city (restaurant site with reservation integration)

**What it is NOT:** App development. BBC builds marketing sites and content sites, not SaaS products or web applications.

---

## S4 — E-Commerce & Booking

**What it is:** Online stores, booking systems, and payment integrations for businesses that transact online.

**When it applies:** Restaurants taking reservations, service businesses accepting bookings, artists selling prints, shops needing a cart.

**What it includes:**
- Booking system integration (OpenTable, Resy, Calendly, custom)
- Payment integration (Stripe, Square)
- Product/menu pages
- Order or inquiry flow

**Example projects:** whatsshakinbacon (catering inquiry form), remodelnow (estimate request flow)

**What it is NOT:** Full e-commerce platforms. For high-volume retail, clients need Shopify. BBC builds custom booking and payment flows on top of existing processors.

---

## S5 — Ongoing Growth

**What it is:** Retainer support — ongoing updates, SEO improvements, analytics review, and site maintenance after launch.

**When it applies:** Clients who want their site to keep improving after launch rather than going stale.

**What it includes:**
- Monthly maintenance (security, performance, dependency updates)
- Content updates (new pages, menu changes, team updates)
- SEO monitoring and improvement
- Analytics review (monthly summary)
- Priority support for fixes

**Example projects:** Ongoing retainers for kris-mid-city, whatsshakinbacon

**What it is NOT:** A subscription to ongoing redesigns. Retainer is for maintenance and incremental improvement, not full rebuilds.

---

## S6 — Digital Strategy

**What it is:** Consulting, audits, and roadmapping for businesses that need clarity on their digital direction before spending on execution.

**When it applies:** Businesses with fragmented digital presence, clients unsure what to build first, or anyone who needs an outside read on what's working and what's not.

**What it includes:**
- Site audit (performance, UX, content gaps)
- Competitor review
- Prioritized recommendations
- Platform or tool recommendations
- Written strategy document

**Example projects:** Pre-project discovery sessions, strategy-only engagements

**What it is NOT:** A replacement for execution. Strategy without build is only useful if the client has another path to execution. Usually leads to an S2+S3 project.
